Story

How it started. How it's going.

It started with curiosity — the kind that makes you take apart a broken device just to see how it works, and then realize you have no idea how to put it back together. That's how I got into code. Not from a class, not from a tutorial — from a problem I needed to solve with no ready answer in sight.

“Build, break, learn, repeat — that loop is the whole story.”

By the time I enrolled in Computer Science & Engineering at Daffodil International University, I'd already built a few things and broken many more. University gave me the vocabulary for what I was already doing — algorithms, compilers, databases — and then I went back to building.

Byte to Byte started as a question: "Can I make one language speak another?" Amber Flow came from being tired of context-switching between five different productivity apps. ISA Connect was for someone who needed it. DBMS was a challenge I set for myself.

Each project taught me something the next one needed.
